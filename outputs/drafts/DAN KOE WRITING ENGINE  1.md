SECTION 3 — MARKET SATURATION MODEL

Referenced concept: Eugene Schwartz market sophistication stages.

Stages summarized:

1. Simple claim market
    
2. Competitive claims
    
3. Mechanism explanation
    
4. Mechanism copying
    
5. Brand differentiation
    

Dan Koe proposes an additional phase:

Stage 6 — Post-information saturation.

Characteristics:

- Info products are commoditized.
    
- Authenticity alone no longer differentiates creators.
    
- Brand + mission + identity become central.
    
- Educational products must evolve into interactive systems.

→ written letters  
→ libraries

- Passive consumption
    
- Lack of real-time feedback
    

New model:

AI-assisted learning environment.

Characteristics:

- interactive
    
- feedback-driven
    
- implementation focused
    
- apprenticeship-like
    

Comparison:

Traditional course

- watch videos
    
- take notes
    
- attempt implementation

- receive feedback
    
- iterate quickly

SECTION 10 — STRATEGIC ADVANTAGE

The article concludes with a key principle:

Competitive advantage is not avoiding AI.

Competitive advantage is applying AI uniquely.

Dan Koe calls this:

Specific knowledge.

Defined as:

- knowledge from genuine curiosity
    
- difficult to train or replicate
    
- accumulated through long experimentation
    
- often at the edge of knowledge
    

Example:

Someone obsessed with productivity builds a unique AI productivity tool.

The uniqueness comes from their experience and insight.

SECTION 11 — COMPETITIVE MOAT

AI lowers technical barriers but increases the value of experience.

Reason:

AI enables anyone to produce content quickly.

However:

Few people possess deep domain experience.

Therefore:

Human taste, insight, and curiosity become differentiators.
