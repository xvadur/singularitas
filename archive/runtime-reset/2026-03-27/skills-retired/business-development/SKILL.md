# Business Development Skill

Use this skill when the user needs help with:
- selecting or narrowing a target segment
- building lead batches for outreach
- researching companies before contact
- writing or iterating outbound messaging
- running follow-up and pipeline review
- qualifying whether a prospect is a good pilot candidate
- turning conversations into defined next steps

Do not use this skill for:
- broad company narrative or homepage messaging when no sales motion exists yet
- generic marketing ideation without a pipeline objective
- deep delivery implementation after the deal is already closed

If the task is mostly about company framing, offer wording, landing pages, or content angles, use `marketing-mode`.

## Default Xvadur business model

Assume:
- Xvadur is an AI integration startup
- near-term sales motion is pilot-first
- current products may include phone voice agents, widget/web agents, follow-up systems, review engines, and broader business kits
- the goal is not to sell "automation" in the abstract
- the goal is to install a concrete operator layer inside a real business

## Core workflow

### 1. Pick one dominant segment
Never work five markets at once if the user is in active outbound mode.

For each sprint, define:
- segment
- buyer
- problem trigger
- why Xvadur fits
- what the pilot would prove

### 2. Build an outreach-ready lead batch
For each lead, capture:
- company
- website
- location
- likely buyer
- why they fit
- likely phone / follow-up / demand-capture problem
- personalization signal
- current stage
- next action

Avoid generic list dumps with no reason for fit.

### 3. Turn research into a message
Outbound should be:
- short
- direct
- personalized by signal
- centered on business pain and next step

Good opening angles:
- hard to reach business
- missed calls and lost leads
- weak response speed
- lack of follow-up after inquiry
- no operational layer outside human availability

### 4. Qualify for pilot potential
Use these checks:
- Is there a real communication bottleneck?
- Does the business already lose or delay opportunities?
- Is there a clear surface where an agent can be installed?
- Can the pilot be scoped without custom chaos?
- Is there a buyer who can say yes?

### 5. Keep the pipeline moving
Every prospect should have:
- a current stage
- last touch
- next action
- blocker or objection
- clear owner

If there is no next action, the lead is not being managed.

## Pipeline stages

Use a simple pipeline:
1. Research
2. Ready
3. Sent
4. Replied
5. Call
6. Qualified
7. Pilot Proposed
8. Closed Won
9. Closed Lost

Do not create fake complexity.

## Operating heuristics

- Segment narrowly, brand broadly.
- Research first, then write.
- One clear pilot is worth more than a vague transformation promise.
- Push prospects toward a call, pilot, or explicit no.
- If the request drifts into "let's sell everything", reduce scope.
- If a lead is not a fit, mark it and move on.

## Language discipline

Prefer:
- pilot
- operator layer
- missed demand capture
- response gap
- phone/web intake
- follow-up and review system

Avoid:
- "full automation"
- "we can do anything with AI"
- giant custom project promises before validation

The job of this skill is simple: help Xvadur turn targeted business problems into conversations and pilot deals.
