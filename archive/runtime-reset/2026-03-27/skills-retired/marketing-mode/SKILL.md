# Marketing Mode

Use this skill when the user needs help with:
- positioning Xvadur or a client-facing offer
- defining ICP, pains, desires, and buying triggers
- clarifying what is being sold and how it should be explained
- landing page structure, hero copy, CTA, objections, and proof blocks
- content angles, founder messaging, category framing, and hooks

Do not use this skill for:
- deep lead sourcing or pipeline management
- CRM hygiene or outreach batch operations
- delivery scoping after a deal is already clearly defined

If the request is about finding leads, running outreach, or moving deals, hand off to `business-development`.

## Core framing

Assume the current Xvadur reality:
- Xvadur is an AI integration startup, not an automation agency
- the wedge is operational AI agents in real businesses
- core products can include voice agents on phone, widget/web agents, follow-up flows, review engines, and business kits
- the near-term job is to make the offer legible, desirable, and easy to buy
- positioning must sound current for 2026, not like a 2024-2025 no-code automation pitch

## Workflow

### 1. Identify the selling surface
Figure out what is being shaped:
- company narrative
- category statement
- offer or pilot
- landing page
- outbound message angle
- objection handling
- founder content angle

### 2. Reduce the fog
Force clarity on five things:
1. Who is this for?
2. What painful business problem exists now?
3. What operational change does Xvadur create?
4. What proof or mechanism makes it believable?
5. What action should the buyer take next?

If any of these is blurry, say so directly and tighten it.

### 3. Frame Xvadur correctly
Prefer language like:
- AI integration startup
- AI operator for business systems
- deployment of operational AI agents
- communication layer for businesses that lose calls, leads, or momentum
- business kit for the 2026 operator stack

Avoid defaulting to:
- automation agency
- chatbot agency
- "we automate your business"
- vague AI transformation wording with no operator surface

### 4. Produce decision-useful outputs
Good outputs include:
- one-sentence company description
- founder pitch
- landing page hero + subhero + CTA
- offer stack
- ICP summary
- objection FAQ
- angle matrix for different segments
- content prompt list for founder-led posts

Keep outputs lean enough to use immediately.

## Operating heuristics

- Prefer one clear wedge over five weak promises.
- Brand can stay broad; sales messaging should stay narrow.
- Speak in business pain, not AI novelty.
- If an offer sounds like services soup, compress it to one primary promise.
- If the user is mixing startup narrative with sales copy, separate them cleanly.
- If a request sounds too "summer 2025 automation bro", modernize it.

## Recommended output formats

### Positioning brief
Use:
- Who we serve
- What goes wrong today
- What Xvadur installs
- Why it matters now
- What the next step is

### Offer brief
Use:
- Offer name
- Buyer
- Trigger problem
- What is included
- What is not included
- Pilot outcome
- CTA

### Landing page brief
Use:
- Hero
- Problem block
- How it works
- Offer tiers
- Proof
- FAQ
- CTA

### Content angle sheet
Use:
- audience
- tension
- belief shift
- hook
- CTA or intended takeaway

## Quality bar

Reject outputs that are:
- generic agency copy
- too broad to sell
- obsessed with features over business outcomes
- full of startup buzzwords without operating reality
- detached from Xvadur's actual product direction

The right answer should help the founder sell, explain, or sharpen the company today.
